Practice Drills

Targeted exercises to sharpen every part of your solve

Untimed inspection

Inspection

Take as long as you need before starting the timer. Plan your full cross and predict as many pairs as you can before touching the cube. This removes the panic of the 15-second window.

How: Solve normally but ignore the inspection limit. Aim to predict a F2L pair more than you usually do.

Slow solves

Lookahead

Solve at half your normal turning speed with zero pauses. Forces you to track the next pair in advance rather than stopping to search.

How: Turn slowly and smoothly. If a pause is coming, slow down further instead of stopping. No pauses allowed.

2-look F2L

Lookahead

Solve two F2L pairs at once — look at two pairs, close your eyes and solve them. This helps to build an intuition of how the pieces move.

How: Solve cross, choose two F2L pairs, close your eyes and solve them. Then repeat for the two next pairs.

Last layer only

TPS & LL

Skip F2L — just practice OLL and PLL back-to-back on a pre-built cube. High repetition builds muscle memory and eliminates hesitation on every case.

How: Build an F2L, then cycle through OLL + PLL repeatedly without re-scrambling. Focus on smooth execution.

PLL time attack

Speed & PLL

Do all 21 PLLs consecutively as fast as you can. This helps your turn speed and PLL execution.

How: Pick an order to do the PLLs in. Then execute them all consecutively at your max speed.

Color neutrality practice

Color neutrality

Practice solving the cross starting on each of the 6 faces from the same scramble. Removes color bias and builds flexible cross recognition for any orientation.

How: Take one scramble and solve the cross on white, yellow, green, blue, red, and orange. Compare times across all faces.
